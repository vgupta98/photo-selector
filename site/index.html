How it works

One decision per moment

A burst of twelve near-identical frames should be one keystroke, not twelve. Rhenium makes it so.

1

Open a folder

Point Rhenium at a folder of JPEG, PNG, HEIC or camera RAW. It scans 30,000+ photos with live progress — no import, no catalog.

2

It groups the duplicates

An on-device AI collapses visually near-identical frames into one tile, with the sharpest shot suggested as your pick. No other open-source culler does this.

3

Cull with one key

Arrow through, press a single key to file the keeper, never reach for the mouse. File into Favourites or any category you like.

Features

Built for culling at scale

Everything a fast cull needs, and nothing that gets in the way.

🧮

On-device similarity grouping

A bundled neural model finds near-identical frames — and a burst detector catches rapid sequences — entirely on your machine.

⌨

Keyboard-first

Arrow keys to move, one key to file, digits for categories. Designed so your hand never leaves the keyboard.

📷

Reads what you shoot

JPEG, PNG, HEIC (the iPhone default) and camera RAW — Canon, Sony, Nikon, Fujifilm, Adobe DNG, Panasonic and Olympus.

📁

Categories & export

Sort into as many flat lists as you like — Selects, Maybes, For Album X. Export a list as text, or copy the photos to a folder.

Pull a group or a hand-picked selection into a focused view — scan them as a grid, then browse full-screen with pan and zoom.

Fast on huge shoots

Cancellable scans, an LRU bitmap cache and a prefetcher keep 30k+ photo folders smooth.

Private by design

100% local. The similarity model — and every pixel it scores — runs entirely on your Mac. No upload, no account, no telemetry. Open source, so you can read exactly what it does.

FAQ

Questions

Is it really free?

Yes. Rhenium is free and open source under the MIT license. No trial, no paywall, no subscription.

Are my photos private?

Completely. Everything runs on your Mac — the similarity model, the decoding, all of it. Nothing is uploaded, there's no account, and there's no telemetry. The source is public so you can verify it.

Why does macOS warn me when I open it?

Because the app isn't notarised by Apple yet — notarisation requires a paid Apple Developer account we're raising funds for. The warning isn't a sign of malware; it's just the absence of a paid Apple signature. Right-click the app and choose Open the first time, and it launches normally after that.

What formats does it read?

JPEG, PNG, HEIC/HEIF, and camera RAW from Canon, Sony, Nikon, Fujifilm, Adobe (DNG), Panasonic and Olympus. HEIC and RAW are decoded through macOS's own image frameworks.

Is there a Windows version?

Not yet — Rhenium is macOS-only today, with Windows support on the roadmap. Star and watch the repo to follow along.
